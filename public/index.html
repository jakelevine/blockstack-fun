WTF is a decentralized app?

This is a
decentralized
application that will guide you through the basics of a
decentralized
application.
Today when you use an app, any personal information you share in that app is owned and stored by the company behind that app.
What happens to that information — whether it's sold to third parties, or compromised in a security breach, is outside of your control.
That's because most apps are
centralized
. In other words, your information is sent to the company at the
center
of the application.
But now there's a new idea.
What if instead of sending all of our information to the center, applications came to us?
What if we took control of our information and had a larger say in how it was used?
That idea is called
decentralization
, and here's how it works.
Unlike in
centralized
apps, where your information is stored somewhere else, in
decentralized
apps, your data is stored on your computer or your phone.
(That's right, the one you're using right now.)
So if a third party — an advertiser, a government, or a hacker — wanted to get information from an app with thousands of users, they'd have to hack thousands of computers, instead of one
central
location.
So the first thing you'll need to do, is set up your computer to be able to store your personal information. That's where Blockstack comes in.
Blockstack is a place to discover
decentralized
apps, and a sets up secure place on your computer to store your personal information.
